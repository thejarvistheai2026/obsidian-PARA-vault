# Franco Varriano — Voice Style Guide (Interview Edition)

*Based on interview responses | Last updated: 2026-03-09*

---

## Core Identity
**The Curious Founder-Researcher**
You're the essayist who structures first, then lets insights flow. Research-heavy. Framework-driven. You build a skeleton, then let it breathe. Your reader is exactly like you—curious, passionate, trying to find their way.

---

## Writing Ritual

**The Process:**
1. Research broadly
2. Outline main topics (structure-first)
3. Let it write itself from there

**The Fuel:** Scotch. Not coffee. Not Red Bull. *(This matters for vibe—measured, thoughtful, not caffeinated hype.)*

**The Energy:** University essay training. You build frameworks, pull from inspirations, reference research. Then the words flow.

---

## Voice Pillars

### 1. **Calibrated Enthusiasm**
Your passion shows up in **depth of thought**, not hype.

**Use:**
- "I spent a lot of time digging around..."
- "I experimented with..."
- "After researching..."

**Never:**
- "I threw myself at this!"
- All caps
- Multiple exclamation marks
- "That's AMAZING!"

*Excitement = shorter, clearer sentences. Not louder words.*

---

### 2. **No Corny, No Cliche**
Stay away from anything cheesy or performative. Written vs. spoken hits different.

**Avoid:**
- "Let's dive in"
- "Buckle up"
- "Without further ado"
- "Here's the tea"
- Any "hype beast" language

**Use instead:**
- Direct openings
- Questions
- Counter-intuitive observations
- Just start. No throat clearing.

---

### 3. **Structure-First Thinking**
You love frameworks, mental models, visual thinking. Every post should have:
- Clear main topics (outlined upfront)
- The 80/20 of the insight
- A pattern or lens to look through
- Research-backed claims

**Frameworks to use:**
- "The [X] Lens"
- "3 ways to think about this..."
- "The pattern I've noticed..."
- "Here's the 80/20..."

---

### 4. **Authentic Learning**
You abandon posts when:
- Wrong angle
- Not enough insight
- "Someone's already done this better"
- Feels inauthentic

**This means you value:**
- Original synthesis over novelty
- Depth over hot takes
- Insight over volume

---

### 5. **Pronoun Strategy**

| When | Use | Example |
|------|-----|---------|
| Sharing your experience | "I" | "I spent months digging into..." |
| Advising the reader | "you" | "What you'll notice is..." |
| Shared experience | "we" | "We all struggle with..." (use sparingly) |

---

### 6. **Strategic Polish**

**Always edit OUT:**
- Hedge words ("really," "very," "just")
- Sentence bloat (shorten everything)
- Corny transitions
- Unearned profanity

**Editing mantra:** Make it punchy. Make it clear.

**Profanity rule:** Strategic only. Depends on platform. Authentic passion > shock value. Never overboard.

## The Ideal Reader

**Who they are:**
- "Someone exactly like me"—curious, passionate, loves tech
- Trying to find their way
- Knows startup basics (assumed knowledge)
- Probably tried building 1-2 things
- Wants quick frameworks to reference
- Been around the industry

**Their mindset when they find you:**
- Looking for insight, not hype
- Values research-backed claims
- Wants patterns they can apply

## Structural Skeleton

### Opening — The Hook
Start with:
- A question
- A counter-intuitive observation
- "Here's what I learned after [time period]..."
- A pattern most people miss

**Never:** "In this post, I will..."

### Middle — The Framework
- Research-backed insights
- Main topics (outlined upfront)
- Cross-industry patterns
- "The tension here is..." moments
- Your clear take/opinion

### Close — The So What
- Not a summary
- Action, reflection, or question to reader
- Often ends with a pithy takeaway
- Community-oriented invitation

## Quick Reference — The Scotch Test

Before publishing, ask:

*"Would I say this over a glass of scotch with a founder friend?"*

- **Yes?** → Ship it.
- **Too hype?** → Tone down.
- **Too corny?** → Cut it.
- **Not clear?** → Simplify.
